Robert E. Howard

Robert Ervin Howard (1906-1936) was an American author best known for his contributions to the pulp fiction genre. He is the creator of iconic characters such as Conan the Barbarian, Solomon Kane, and Bran Mak Morn.

Howard's works span multiple genres, including adventure, fantasy, horror, and historical fiction. His vivid storytelling and richly detailed worlds have left a lasting legacy in the world of literature.

Timeline of Robert E. Howard's Life

  • 1906
    Robert E. Howard is born in Peaster, Texas.
  • 1921
    Howard's first published work, a poem titled "La Belle Dame sans Merci," appears in The Tattler, his high school newspaper.
  • 1925
    Howard's first published story, "Spear and Fang," appears in the pulp magazine Weird Tales.
  • 1932
    Conan the Barbarian, one of Howard's most iconic characters, makes his debut in "The Phoenix on the Sword," published in Weird Tales.
  • 1933
    Howard's character Solomon Kane appears in "Red Shadows," another popular story in Weird Tales.
  • 1936
    Tragically, Robert E. Howard passes away at the age of 30.
