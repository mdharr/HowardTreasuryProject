Robert E. Howard

Robert Ervin Howard (1906-1936) was an American author best known for his contributions to the pulp fiction genre. He is the creator of iconic characters such as Conan the Barbarian, Solomon Kane, and Bran Mak Morn.

Howard's works span multiple genres, including adventure, fantasy, horror, and historical fiction. His vivid storytelling and richly detailed worlds have left a lasting legacy in the world of literature.
